Maintanence


Light

Let cacti soak up the sun—but not too much—and enjoy the heat. You want things to be just right for your prickly plant. "Cacti enjoy all the attention they can get from the sun and thrive in the direct light—place them in the warmest, sunniest corner of your home"


A south or west-facing window with a few hours of direct sunlight works well. In low-light conditions, use a grow light to supplement natural lighting. Palomares also advises keeping cacti away from air conditioner vents or drafty windows since they enjoy warm, dry climates.



Water

Give your cactus a good drink when it's thirsty, with long breaks in between. After watering, empty excess water from the saucer, which can lead to rot.


"The reason most people fail with cacti is the tendency to over-water, which can lead to root rot and to scab, which appears as rusty-colored, corky areas on the stems," Thon says. "My advice is to err on the side of under-watering; most of the time you can bring them back from the dehydration stage with no issues."



soil

Using a soil mix tailored to cacti and other succulents is the key to a healthy plant. Regular potting soil contains more organic matter and retains too much moisture. "Cacti prefer soil that includes more sand and rocks, in order to drain water and keep the plant dry between drinks and prevent root rot,"



Fertilizer

As for fertilizer, choose one specifically made for cacti or succulents, which will be low in nitrogen and higher in phosphorus and potassium. Dilute and apply according to the directions on the label.

Benifits


Low Maintenance:


Cacti are hardy plants that require minimal care. They thrive in dry conditions and need infrequent watering, making them perfect for busy individuals or those new to gardening.

Air Purification:


Like other houseplants, cacti absorb carbon dioxide and release oxygen, improving air quality. Some species also help filter indoor pollutants.

Stress Reduction:


Having plants around can reduce stress and promote a sense of calm. Cacti, with their unique shapes and vibrant flowers, can create a soothing environment.

Aesthetic Appeal:


Cacti come in a variety of shapes, sizes, and colors, adding a unique and exotic touch to any space. They can be used as striking decorative elements in homes and offices.

Educational Value:


Growing cacti can be a fun and educational hobby. Learning about different species, their growth habits, and care requirements can be both rewarding and informative.
